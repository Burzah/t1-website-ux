Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti is a small Pacific island with a land area of less than 500 square miles. It has a population of approximately 20,000 residents. The island features a mix of beaches, rainforests, and a small active volcano, making it both scenic and diverse.

Taniti uses 120-volt outlets, the same as in the United States. No adapter is needed for U.S. travelers.

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, though it is not strictly enforced.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in tourist areas. English is less common in rural regions, particularly among older residents.

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital is well-equipped and staffed with multilingual employees.

Violent crime is very rare. However, with increased tourism, incidents of pickpocketing and petty theft have risen slightly. Stay aware in crowded areas.

Taniti observes many national holidays. On those days, tourist attractions, restaurants, and some businesses may be closed, so plan accordingly.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Credit cards are widely accepted, and banks offer currency exchange services.
